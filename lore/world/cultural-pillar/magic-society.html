MAGIC-SOCIETY

Magical Societies

1. What is Expendable?

  • Individuals.
  • Souls.
  • Long-term sanity.
  • Static traditions.
  • Weak bloodlines.

2. What Must Be Preserved at All Costs?

  • Magic.
  • Controlled territories.
  • Sacrifice reserves.
  • Cinders.
  • Youthful vigor

3. Where Does Authority Sit?

  • Decentralized, merit-based, brutal.
  • Spell-weavers hold battlefield command.
  • Cinders manage ritual authority and provide prophetic guidance.
  • No eternal rulers. Tenures end in burnout or challenge.
  • Leadership shifts with magical potency.

4. How Do They Justify Survival?

  • "Better to burn fast than be erased slowly."
  • The Mist devours the timid; fire claims the bold.
  • Cinder magic is the world's true pulse - stasis invites Hollowing.
  • Every flare extends humanity's defiant light.

5. Governance Follows Doctrine

Civilian Life

  • Fatalistic youth-heavy society.
  • Reveres those who burn brightly.
  • Conscription from adolescence; training in pain tolerance.
  • Relationships forged in ritual circles, dissolved by burnout.
  • These states normalize Hollowing.

Social Structure

  • Identity tied to cinder-mark (personal magic scar, acquired or branded on at birth).
  • Marriages as power alliances (merging lines of powerful casters).

Economy

  • Trade in crystals, soul-essences, scrolls.
  • Opportunistic raids on Husk clusters for magical reagents.
  • Currency secondary to raw potency (measured in burn-rate)

Combat Doctrine

  • Aggressive flare-strikes, never prolonged defenses.
  • Hit-and-burn: ignite, retreat, repeat.
  • Drag foes into cinder-zones for mutual Hollowing.
  • Weaponize burnout with suicide spells as last resort.

Weaknesses

  • Burnout epidemics (mass Hollowing cascades).
  • Youth dependency, little age-granted wisdom.
  • Internal purges (challenges fracture unity).
