NOMAD-SOCIETY

⚙️ Scavenger Clans / Iron Nomads

1. What is expendable?

  • Territory
  • Settlements
  • Outsiders
  • Material surplus
  • The weak who slow the caravan

2. What must be preserved at all costs?

  • Vehicles (these are lineage, inheritance, identity)
  • Bloodlines / crews
  • Routes and path-knowledge
  • Mobility itself

3. Where does authority sit?

  • Decentralized, vehicle-based
  • Authority lies with:
    • Lead drivers (navigation authority)
    • Mechanics (survival authority)
    • Path-readers / Mist-sensitives (mythic authority)
      No “king.” At best:
  • Convoy leaders (temporary, situational)
  • Leadership changes based on context:
    • Travel = driver leads
    • Breakdown = mechanic leads
    • Danger = path-reader leads
      Authority shifts mid-scene depending on the problem.

4. How do they justify survival?

  • “Standing still is death.”
  • “The world belongs to what moves through it.”
  • “We don’t fight the Mist - we outlive it.”
  • “Settlers choose their graves. We don’t.”

5. Governance follows doctrine

Civilian life:

  • No permanent homes → everything modular, collapsible, strapped down
  • Children raised on the move, taught roles early
  • Relationships are practical first, emotional second

Social structure:

  • Identity = your vehicle + your crew
  • Marriages = merging crews / resources
  • Exile = being left behind (worse than death)

Economy:

  • Trade is opportunistic:
    • salvage
    • fuel
    • parts
    • information about routes
  • Currency is secondary to utility

Mist / Husk integration

Practical layer:

  • They read:
    • wind shifts in the Mist
    • sound distortion
    • Husk movement rhythms

Ritualized layer:

  • Engine rhythms used as signal patterns
  • Horns, chants, or drumbeats to guide or disperse Husks
  • Smoke mixtures that mask or redirect presence

Social consequence:

  • Path-readers are:
    • respected
    • feared
    • possibly "other"

Weaknesses

  • Dependence on vehicles → breakdown = crisis
  • Limited heavy infrastructure → can’t hold ground
  • Internal fragmentation → clans may not cooperate
  • Resource bottlenecks (fuel, parts, specialists)
    Optional nasty one:
  • Aging problem → older members become liabilities if they can’t keep up
