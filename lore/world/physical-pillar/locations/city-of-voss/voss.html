Cobbler's Road

Cobbler's Road sprawls beyond the northern walls, where city authority becomes more of a suggestion than a reality. Cheap housing, struggling businesses, workshops, and temporary shelters dominate the district. It attracts laborers, debtors, drifters, and anyone who cannot afford life inside the walls. Fuct Avenue forms its miserable heart, serving as a monument to bad decisions and worse luck. Lonely Alley cuts through the district's interior, providing privacy for meetings nobody wants witnessed. Cobbler's Road is poor, overcrowded, and frequently overlooked.

Lowgate

Lowgate hugs the northern wall, living permanently beneath the shadow of Last Chance High Security Prison. Guards, prison workers, and families of inmates make up much of the district's North-side population. The Awkward Guard Café serves as an unofficial gathering place for correctional staff, while the Wet Goose provides a less respectable alternative. The Trusty Grape handles much of the district's business dealings, both legal and otherwise. Security presence is heavy, though residents have long since stopped noticing armed patrols. Visitors often find Lowgate oppressive, but locals consider it simply predictable and strict.

Black Topper

Black Topper serves as the city's commercial workhorse, the name being a reference to the black caps used for cans of diesel. Warehouses, wholesalers, delivery depots, retail shops, and service businesses crowd its streets from dawn until well after dark. The Golden Boar Drugstore supplies half the city with medicine, while the Humble Carrot's delivery network keeps goods moving between districts. Clothes Contact Mall draws merchants, workers, and suppliers from nearly every corner of the city. Traffic is constant, if something needs to be bought, shipped, repaired, or acquired on short notice, Black Topper is usually the answer. Few residents describe the district as beautiful, but almost everyone depends on its services.

Clay-Knot

Clay-Knot sits near the geographical and political center of the city. Emerald Street showcases the city's most prestigious businesses, while Boulderkeep Detention Center quietly demonstrates how differently justice can function for different people, and the Redemption Low-Sec Prison provides a more optimistic face to incarceration than most citizens are accustomed to. The Great Hearth Church maintains a powerful presence throughout the district, funding services, taking care of neighborhoods, and recruiting new followers daily. The Crystal Quill Casino attracts professionals, officials, and ambitious social climbers. More important decisions are made in Clay-Knot than anywhere else in the city.

Greenhill

Greenhill is where influence prefers to appear respectable. The BIG Feather dominates the local skyline and reputation alike, with its reporters constantly digging into matters somebody would rather keep buried. The Happy Gun serves hunters, security personnel, and private citizens alike, though opinions differ on whether its cheerful branding is amusing or disturbing. Compared to neighbouring districts, Greenhill feels orderly and well-maintained, with the majority of the middle class being housed here. Political conversations are common here, despite a lack of politicians.

  • c. Teal Highway
  • i. The Happy Gun
  • w. The BIG Feather

Watcher's Walk

Watcher's Walk lies beyond the southern walls along the riverbanks, where the city gradually gives way to water, industry, and the Mist. Docks, warehouses, boarding houses, and riverside businesses dominate the landscape. The Weeping Gull serves as a gathering place for sailors, dockworkers, and anyone seeking information arriving from beyond the city. Dead Walk passes through the district, adding an atmosphere many residents have simply learned to live with. Further downstream sits Cinder Harbor, a place whose reputation grows darker with every retelling. Along the bridges and riverbanks lies the subdistrict of Bounty Bridge, where bounty hunters, debt collectors, and other professionals of flexible morality frequently conduct business. Watcher's Walk rarely sleeps. The river ensures that something is always moving, and the Mist tends to keep people awake.

  • d. Dead Walk
  • h. The Weeping Gull
  • n. The Cinder Harbor

Wolfkey

Wolfkey occupies the south-eastern portion of the city and has earned a reputation as a place where people go when they do not wish to be found. Narrow alleys, interconnected courtyards, hidden entrances, and aging tenements create a maze-like environment that confuses outsiders. The Crafty Carpet Store conducts its suspiciously successful business here, while the Talon Bar attracts mercenaries and bounty hunters from across the region. The Onyx remains Wolfkey's greatest mystery, drawing influential visitors without ever explaining why. The district contains countless safehouses, backroom operations, and businesses that avoid being thought about too long. Criminals, informants, investigators, and legitimate residents coexist in uneasy balance. Wolfkey is not lawless, but it sure as hell walks that line.
