What is Ember Deck?

Said plainly, Ember Deck is a Dieselpunk TTRPG Deckbuilder made for (ideally) 3-4 Player groups, but it should still scale relatively well until about 5-6 people. It was an idea heavily inspired by gems such as "Slay The Spire", "Hearthstone", "Slice and Dice", and "Morimens".
The gameplay features tight resource management in a low-math, high-keyword environment, giving a couple of anchor points to make Your own character and their deck unique and powerful, whereas the Setting provides plenty of opportunity for "angsty" or "against all odds" roleplay in a grimdark world, where magic is ubiquitous, but hope is scant.

Summary of the Setting

Once, there was HELL.
Every day, souls passed in and out, getting processed, stripped bare, and sent to the Hall of Echoes, pure.
One day an apocalyptic event happened.
Because of that, HELL is working overtime and is still decades behind.
Because of that, Souls get clogged up in the in-between, and the radiation that results from this pileup seeps into the living, and covers the world under a blanket of mist.
Until finally, their bodies or minds can't take it anymore, and burn up, or hollow out, from the inside.

Character Creation

Once a concept of who you'd like to play as forms in your mind, the preferable place to go is the CHARACTER>OATHS Section, where you'll find all three major Oaths (which function like classes, and determine your starting Attributes - Prisms), these in turn determine your Starting Deck.
All Oaths start with 10 BACKBONE cards making up their deck, but the keywords may vary based on chosen Oath and even subtle differences between the same Oath's decks are possible at times.
Now, having a concept, embodied by an Oath, and a Deck, you select your CHARGE, and your Passives (Each Oath offers a different selection). These are the anchor points mentioned before - they'll define your playstyle heavily.
Once that's in order, and your friends have done the same, you're set!
(Don't forget to send at least 4 sentences worth of a backstory to your GM, though.)

Playing the Game

As with most RPGs, the weight of this point lands mostly on the GM, however some things to keep in mind as a Player skipping through sections:

  • Checks are made with d6's, many of them at times (up to 8-ish).
  • Miniatures are welcome but leave the rulers at home - combat doesn't use movement.
  • Agree on a vision you and the other players have on your bond, how you met and connected, it will save the GM a couple of grey hairs.
  • Enjoy!
