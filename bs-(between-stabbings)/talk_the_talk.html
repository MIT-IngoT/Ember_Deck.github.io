TALK_THE_TALK

Social system

  • Affinity (1-10) is the long-term relationship state, hidden from players and used as the underlying modifier.
    • Affinity can be earned or lost depending on player actions and/or reputation. Each of the examples given can also be reversed to achieve the opposite effect. It usually moves in increments of 1-2 points at a time, dependent on campaign length.
    • Positive Actions:
      • Fulfilling an NPC's request (ex. Doing the job you're hired for)
      • Siding with them in an argument (ex. Helping the barman rid of an out of control drunk)
      • Doing something they benefit from (ex. Shutting down a local mafioso that was threatening them - Important! The Players do not need to know how and who they're benefitting when taking an action to still be viewed positively)
    • Negative Actions:
      • Siding with the opposite side of the argument (ex. Joining the aforementioned drunkard)
      • Stealing from or harming the NPC directly (ex. Injuring the barman when a fight erupts between patrons)
      • Doing something that affects them negatively (ex. letting a cult blow up their house by being late to the scene - Important! The NPC has to know about something to be upset over it)
  • Patience is a derived scene clock: Affinity × an NPC/situation-specific multiplier (1-5).
    • This multiplier is entirely in the hands of the GM, but as a rough reference guide:
      • 1–2: distracted, busy, hostile environment
      • 3: normal conversation
      • 4–5: relaxed, safe, or invested
  • Players do not see the scores themselves. They only get narrative clues, shifts in tone, body language, and response quality.
  • Insight can dampen Patience damage, so socially skilled characters can prolong or soften conversations.
  • When Patience runs out, the NPC ends the exchange, refuses further pressure, or forces the scene to close.

Insight dampening

  • Base Patience damage per investigative question/request: 6
  • Minor provocation / repetitive pressure: 9
  • Major push / insult / contradiction: 12
    Insight dampens this DMG by its raw value, but cannot reduce it below 1.

Pivot

Once per exchange, when the players introduce new, relevant leverage / a meaningful concession / impactful information, the tone of the conversation may shift. If the leverage is significant, Patience loss from the next interaction is reduced (bring the DMG down one level 12->9->6->1), and the NPC’s response changes accordingly.
Repeated or low-value attempts have no effect, or may even insult the NPC. A pivot must do at least one of these:

  • Change the stakes (“If you don’t help, this gets worse for you too”)
  • Reveal something relevant (not trivia, actual leverage)
  • Offer real cost (risk, debt, obligation—not pocket change)
  • Reframe the situation (make the NPC reconsider their position)

Last Chance — Foot in the Door

When Patience would reach 0, the players may attempt one final appeal: [offer leverage / make a meaningful concession]. If the offer is compelling, the NPC allows one additional exchange and may adjust Affinity [positively / negatively]. If it fails, the conversation ends immediately and Affinity is reduced.
The offer must be one of:

  • Value (money, item, favour)
  • Information (useful, relevant, new)
  • Commitment (promise with cost or risk)
    If it’s not one of those, it’s just stalling.

Quirks

NPC's can also receive Quirks if they're relevant enough or just appear often in a game or its segment. These are additional modifiers to the rules above so use them sparingly. As a Player, feel free to add nuance to your character by borrowing some of these, maybe even in combination!
