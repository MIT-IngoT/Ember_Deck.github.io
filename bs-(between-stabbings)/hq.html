Headquarters / Hideout

Long jobs pay off

Long-distance jobs improve the HQ through a staying-behind NPC. The HQ is a place that evolves because the party neglects it, which is a bit of a reversal: being away has an opportunity cost, but also generates progress through caretaking, repairs, or investment of locals the Hearth may have previously helped/hired.

  • Use travel time as the main variable, named when giving out the assignment. 
    • Example: Near, Local, Far, Remote, and Expedition; each band costs different amounts of in-world time to travel to and return (ex. 1h / 3h / 6h / 12h / 24h+, respectively).
    • Danger is tied to travel time. Near HQ, the return is easy; farther away, the route becomes slower, more dangerous, or more event-heavy.
  • HQ effects scale by time band. Longer absences should produce stronger HQ gains, better caretaker progress, or larger downtime upgrades.
    • Long travel creates risk or upkeep cost. Time spent away can also create pressure: supplies consumed, HQ neglected, opportunities lost, or complications on return.
    • Each 8-hour segment gives the Caretaker one hidden d6 roll. Keep the mechanic behind the screen, only revealing the results once the players arrive back at base.
      • Matching dice results create Upgrade Pips. Any pair of identical results in the same batch counts as one point toward HQ facility progress.
      • The improvement that triggers is chosen by the matching value. 1-2 Gives a pip for the Quarters. 3-4 Gives a pip for the Workshop, and 5-6 gives a pip to the Archive.
      • The more dice rolled, the more likely a match becomes, but the rarer perfect uniform rolls stay. That creates rising odds without making the system predictable.
    • Facility progress only happens at thresholds. Upgrade Points do not directly become effects; they unlock discrete facility upgrades once a required total is reached.
      • Ruined - 5 pips -> Basic
      • Basic - 4 pips -> Improved
      • Improved - 5 pips -> Advanced
      • Advanced - 6 pips -> State of the Art

Tiering model and Facilities

For the tiering:

  • Ruined: not built yet.
  • Basic: simple, modest effect.
  • Improved: the signature effect that makes the facility distinct
  • Advanced: Restoring Backbone Decay between expeditions for the facility's chosen Prism.
  • State of the Art: +1 to a given Prism's Signature Backbone capacity
    Facilities can start prebuilt! Some HQ areas may begin Ruined, Basic, or even Improved depending on fiction, so the GM should allow a ready-made medbay, arsenal, or archive when the story calls for it.
    If you want some HQs to begin partially built, then “Ruined -> Basic” can just be the first step for places that start as ruins or blank shells, while established bases begin at Basic or Improved depending on lore.

Rest Site / Medical bay / Quarters (1-2)

  • Basic: remove leftover statuses from the last expedition.
  • Improved: restore a single point of Prism loss after a Trauma hit.
  • Advanced: restore decayed Backbone bonus on Guile cards for the next expedition.
  • State of the Art: protect one Guile (per Ember) card from Backbone decay permanently.

Armoury / Arsenal / Workshop (3-4)

  • Basic: gain a random Tool before each expedition (+1 Per Tier).
  • Improved: Gain 1 Shard before each expedition (+1 at max Tier).
  • Advanced: restore decayed Backbone bonus on Might cards for the next expedition.
  • State of the Art: protect one Might (per Ember) card from Backbone decay permanently.

Sanctum / Archive / Library (5-6)

  • Basic: Access to encountered enemy Loops and Doctrine Library
  • Improved: carry one Doctrine from your last expedition onto the next one (Max Tier: does not count against the standard amount of Doctrines per expedition).
  • Advanced: restore decayed Backbone bonus on Insight cards for the next expedition.
  • State of the Art: protect one Insight card (per Ember) from Backbone decay permanently.
